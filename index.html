I am a research scientist at MIT Lincoln Laboratory, where I am developing algorithms for machine learning-aided decision support for U.S. DoD and Homeland Security.

I also collaborate with Finale Doshi-Velez, Harvard University, who advised the research for my Master's Thesis and who I intend to work under as I complete my graduate education. I work in the fields of reinforcement learning, machine learning, and Bayesian deep learning. I have long been interested in decision making and the mechanisms by which humans summarize and reason about the world. In my work, I aim to develop models and algorithms that enable actors (whether human or not) to efficiently make decisions in the face of various forms of uncertainty.

Previous to joining MIT LL and enrolling at Harvard, I had the privilege of working with Tadd Truscott as a founding member of the Splash Lab at Brigham Young University.
